Global Digital Solutions Co-Op

In this role I began by completing a month long Angular 12 training to begin my front-end development career. Once completed I assisted in several projects updating UI/UX over several pages. Towards the end of the term I competed in the Think.Make.Start competition, an internal competition to bring a collaboration of associates on different problems to find new innovations and solutions to help the company. As a new project had begun to take form I had been brought in to discuss a plan of attack as we would need to create an access point for users on the floor with the approach to use Near Field Communication (NFC) tags as the contact. I immediately found interest in this as I found I may be able to apply some of my electrical engineering background to this project, so I along with another intern were tasked with doing research and looking into options. After understanding how YubiKey authenticators worked and utilized NFC, I found that I would be able to use simple queries and our database namiing system to create this link between the NFC tag and the user. The last piece of this puzzle was to understand mobile device compatibility and options including form submission and camera uploads. In addition to this research I also created several demos to test and explore mobile device compatibility with our application and use cases. In the Think.Make.Start. competition I served as a lead developer on an Oracle Apex site which was used to create a inventory management system, a notification system for down time, a new form which would condense the current process from seven into one, and created a better tracking system for upper management to review progress. On the side of my main job functions, I worked with a few interns on a new intern onboarding application which would make it easier for managers, interns, and intern management to be able to onboard and check the status of the process. Lastly, I worked with my team and department managers to create a tracking system for available time throughout the year incorporating project estimates, vacations, and office closure. This system had initially been proposed as an application to which I would be developing so I created an arcitectural diagram, fully immersive wireframe, and met with our cloud team to understand data and storage options. Ultimately this project was canceled and became an excel sheet which I used several macros to create an interactive visualization tool to view availability. In this role I learned new skills including Angular, networking and profesionalism, Oracle Apex, Oracle Sql, VBA, Agile Project Mangement Methodoloigies, how to understand a process from different perspectives, effective communication skills, and more.
